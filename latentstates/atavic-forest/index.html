Atavic Forest

2022–2023 · Champalimaud Foundation, Mucho Flow Festival
Lunar Ring · Jonathan Uliel Saldanha · Gonçalo Guiomar · Zachary Mainen
This forest has been fully dreamt by Artificial Intelligence. Hidden within the corpus of its training data lie forgotten memories that diffuse through a fluid continuum. Throughout our evolutionary path, our genes have kept a record of our relationship with nature, hiding it in order to avoid unnecessary expression. This installation is an expression of that hidden knowledge — an exploration of what a space of these memories would look like if we could directly interface with them in a pre-linguistic stance; direct contact with alterity.

The Atavic Forest exists wholly in latent space. Each frame is generated in real-time by neural networks trained on over 180,000 forest images. No pixel is photographed; every surface, every branch, every play of light is hallucinated by the model and rendered live during a 45-minute performance. Collective movement in the room steers the journey through an ancient forest — the machine attempts to interpret and amplify what is being felt and seen.

Sound Architecture

Jonathan Uliel Saldanha — composition, 7.2 surround

Sound is not accompaniment but architecture. Saldanha’s 7.2 surround composition feeds directly into the generative layers, perturbing the networks themselves. Sonic vibrations reshape the topology of the latent space, opening passages and sealing others. The forest responds to sound as much as to motion — auditory and visual experience fuse into a single sensory field.

Mucho Flow — Ten Generative Worlds

October 2023 · Guimarães, Portugal

For Mucho Flow Festival, the system expanded from a single forest to ten generative worlds. Stable Diffusion generated new training sets, which were recompressed through specialized GANs — each world its own hallucinatory landscape. Fungal networks serve as portals between spaces: reactor spores growing in Chernobyl-blue light, thermal fire rendered in inverted infrared, toxic mycelium glowing with alien bioluminescence. Transitions between worlds use latent blending, exploiting change blindness to create imperceptible passages.
