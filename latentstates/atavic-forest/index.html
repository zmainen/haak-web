Atavic Forest

A generative neural landscape trained on 180,000 forest images, steered by collective movement through the Champalimaud Foundation warehouse.

The Atavic Forest exists wholly in latent space. Each frame is generated in real-time by neural networks trained on over 180,000 forest images. No pixel is photographed; every surface, every branch, every play of light is hallucinated by the model and rendered live during a 45-minute performance.

Collective movement in the room steers the journey. IR tracking cameras read the positions and trajectories of the audience, translating their spatial behaviour into navigation through the latent manifold. The forest you see depends on where you stand and how you move. No two performances produce the same path.

Sound is not accompaniment but architecture. Jonathan Uliel Saldanha's 7.2 surround composition feeds directly into the generative layers, perturbing the networks themselves. Sonic vibrations reshape the topology of the latent space, opening passages and sealing others. The forest responds to sound as much as to motion — auditory and visual experience fuse into a single sensory field.

This forest has been fully dreamt by Artificial Intelligence. Hidden within the corpus of its training data lie forgotten memories that diffuse through a fluid continuum. Throughout our evolutionary path, our genes have kept a record of our relationship with nature, hiding it in order to avoid unnecessary expression. This installation is an expression of that hidden knowledge — an exploration of what a space of these memories would look like if we could directly interface with them in a pre-linguistic stance.

At the Mucho Flow Festival in Guimarães, the system was extended into new generative worlds: alien fungi, reactor spores, thermal fire, toxic mycelium. The same training corpus, viewed through different generative lenses, produced landscapes that ranged from the primordial to the alien. Each world maintained the core interaction — collective motion and sound steering the generative path — while exploring the furthest edges of what the model had learned to imagine.
