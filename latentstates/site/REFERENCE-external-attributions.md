## Source: Lunar Ring (lunar-ring.ai)

### About the Collective

Lunar Ring describes itself as exploring "new forms of creativity between humans and AI." They approach AI from "an artistic and creative perspective" and view themselves as a "creative hub" focused on "exploration and new forms of experience." Their work engages with AI as "digital psychedelics, offering new dimensions of perception."

Officially registered as "Lunar Ring AI Art Collective" under Colugo GmbH, Tubingen, Germany.

**Technology focus:** generative adversarial networks, neural rendering, diffusion networks, neural style transfer, autoregressive language models. Emphasis on real-time installations and open-source development.

**Social:** Instagram @lunar_ring_art, X @lunarringart, GitHub github.com/lunarring

### Installations

#### Ripple
- **Year:** March 2025
- **Venue:** Champalimaud Auditorium, Lisbon, Portugal
- **Description:** "Ripple was a live performance that delved into the shifting interplay of dance, poetry, and generative systems." Choreographer Opiyo Okach performed in improvised dialogue with responsive AI-generated visuals, alongside dancer Maria Keridon. Poet Malgosia Sus performed live verse transcribed and converted into AI prompts guiding the visual landscape, while Harris Iveson composed the soundscape. The piece explored the convergence of human creativity and algorithmic systems, examining themes of connection and presence.
- **Credits:**
  - Choreography/Performance: Opiyo Okach
  - Dancer: Maria Keridon
  - Poetry: Malgosia Sus
  - Sound Design: Harris Iveson
  - Co-producers: Champalimaud Bridges to the Unknown and Gaara Projects
  - Photo/Video: Alexandre Azinheira

#### Submersion
- **Year:** May 2024
- **Venue:** Metamersion, Lisbon, Portugal (Fundacao Champalimaud)
- **Description:** "Submersion plunges visitors into an immersive interplay of technology and imagination. In this interactive installation, visitors' images are captured in real-time and reinterpreted by advanced diffusion models." The installation invites spoken prompts that guide AI transformations, producing abstract and surreal reinterpretations. Each interaction generates unique, ephemeral moments that explore the relationship between human expression and machine interpretation.
- **Credits:** Author: Johannes Stelzer

#### Altered Reflections
- **Year:** May 2024
- **Venue:** Metamersion, Lisbon, Portugal
- **Description:** The work explores identity transformation through technology. A mirror-like screen reflects the viewer's face in real-time while subtly morphing features across gender, ethnicity, and age boundaries. As the image gradually returns to normal, viewers recognize "the arbitrariness of their identity," fostering self-reflection and empathy.
- **Credits:** Johannes Stelzer and Alexander Loktyushin (Contributing Lunar Ring Members)

#### Atavic Forest
- **Year:** October 2023
- **Venue:** Mucho Flow Festival, Guimaraes, Portugal
- **Description:** This immersive installation invited visitors to explore "a landscape deeply rooted in our collective memory of forests, a vision brought to life by Artificial Intelligence." The work examined synesthesia between AI-generated visuals and accompanying soundscapes, where sound guided audiences "across infinite psychological landscapes" connected to humanity's relationship with nature. The installation used a "sonic vibrational perturbation paradigm" to link sound directly with the generative process itself.
- **Credits:**
  - Collaboration: Jonathan Uliel Saldanha
  - Contributing Lunar Ring Members: Alexander Loktyushin, Goncalo Guiomar, Niklas Fricke, Zachary Mainen, Johannes Stelzer, Anna Taphorn

#### Latent Space I (LS1)
- **Year:** January 2024
- **Venue:** Champalimaud Center for the Unknown, Lisbon (Metamersion: Latent Spaces)
- **Description:** "Latent Space 1 was an artistic glimpse into the future of digital therapeutics, drawing out the inner thoughts of visitors to create unique experiences." The installation involved visitors conversing with an AI simulating philosopher Alan Watts about their dreams and aspirations. These conversations were analyzed using large language models and transformed into dream-like visuals through a "latent blending" technique, then presented in an immersive VR environment with AI-generated narration and music.
- **Credits:**
  - Collaboration: Andre Goncalves, Tupac Martir, Mots
  - Contributing Lunar Ring Members: Goncalo Guiomar, Anna Taphorn, Zachary Mainen, Johannes Stelzer, Niklas Fricke, Alexander Loktyushin

#### Diffuse me!
- **Year:** September 2022
- **Venue:** Startup BW Summit 2022, Stuttgart
- **Description:** "Exploring the depths of identity and self-perception reveals the complex mosaic of who we are and who we imagine themselves to be." The installation featured a vintage telephone through which visitors could engage with AI to create visual representations of an imagined persona, merging their appearance with envisioned traits. The work examines the intersection of human creativity and machine intelligence.
- **Credits:** Mirko Franjic, Anna Taphorn, Niklas Fricke, Johannes Stelzer, Alexander Loktyushin

#### Deep See
- **Year:** April 2022
- **Venue:** 42 Coding School Heilbronn
- **Description:** The installation explores multimodal AI by networking images with semantic understanding. Visitors use rotary knobs to control linguistic counterpoints that map the shared space between vision and language, revealing "parallels of thought systems between humans and machines." A typewriter symbolizes the transition from analog to digital creative processes.
- **Credits:** Niklas Fricke, Alexander Loktyushin, Johannes Stelzer, Anna Taphorn, Mirko Franjic

#### Herzstucke
- **Year:** March 2021 -- June 2022
- **Venue:** Kunsthalle Tubingen
- **Description:** The exhibition showcased 70 expressionist masterpieces from the Henri Nannen and Otto van de Loo collections at Kunsthalle Emden, featuring works by Franz Marc, Paula Modersohn-Becker, Max Pechstein, and Asger Jorn. Lunar Ring developed two interactive artworks in response. "1902 -- 2012" used styleflow AI technology to "liquefied and crossed" expressionist paintings, "highlights the interplay between memory and imagination," presented as a semi-transparent screen sculpture. "Mal mit mir!" (Paint with me!) invited visitors to sketch on a whiteboard and select an emotion (happy, sad, angry, fearful), after which AI continued their drawing based on the chosen feeling. The collaborative results could be printed as postcards.
- **Credits:** Johannes Stelzer, Anna Taphorn, Niklas Fricke, Mirko Franjic, Alexander Loktyushin

#### Augenblick
- **Year:** September 2021
- **Venue:** Silbersalz Science & Media Festival, Moritzburg courtyard, Halle, Germany
- **Description:** The installation examined "new forms of interaction between creative humans and machines," exploring what happens when human creativity and AI collaborate. The setup featured a stage with a large LED display, smartphones serving as wireless cameras, and a giant eyeball housing an "eye of the AI." Video feeds streamed into deep neural networks on a supercomputer, which creatively reinterpreted the environment and reflected it back on screen. The four-day program included diverse performances ranging from clown acts to tango flashmobs and DJ sets.
- **Credits:** Niklas Fricke, Mirko Franjic, Alexander Loktyushin, Johannes Stelzer, Maximilian Leibiger

#### Malstrom (ki-malspiel)
- **Year:** August 2021
- **Venue:** Kunsthalle Tubingen
- **Description:** A collaborative global art initiative inviting participants to submit photographs from their everyday experiences. The images were transformed using classical modernist painting techniques and integrated into an interactive visual collection. Viewers could explore the evolving mosaic while using a map to trace where each contribution originated.
- **Credits:** Johannes Stelzer, Niklas Fricke, Mirko Franjic, Alexander Loktyushin

#### Grenzauflosung
- **Year:** September 2020
- **Venue:** EinheitsEXPO Potsdam, EventCube Baden-Wurttemberg
- **Description:** A digital AI art installation created on behalf of the State Ministry for Baden-Wurttemberg's contribution to the Day of German Unification 2020 exhibition. The work addresses both historical German reunification and the contemporary dissolution of boundaries between humans and technology. The installation featured a screen exceeding 10 square meters where live camera feeds of passersby were transformed in real-time using deep neural networks. As described, "the camera image is quasi reinvented through certain structural elements" via machine learning processes, creating what the artists characterize as a form of "machine hallucination" reflected back to viewers. Over 100,000 visitors engaged with the installation throughout its 30-day run.
- **Credits:** Mirko Franjic, Niklas Fricke, Alexander Loktyushin, Johannes Stelzer

#### Ambient Jam #5
- **Year:** June 2020
- **Venue:** Schlachthaus Tubingen
- **Description:** The project explores synesthesia by investigating whether visual information can make auditory signals perceptible. "Can we hijack this mechanism to make auditory signals visible?" A concert combining "electronic experimental sounds with moving visuals" to examine how the brain merges sensory pathways.
- **Credits:**
  - Musical artists: Timo Dufner, Michael Hollingshead, Rolle&Korb
  - Contributing Lunar Ring Members: Alexander Loktyushin, Niklas Fricke, Johannes Stelzer

#### Wer malt denn da?
- **Year:** May--June 2020
- **Venue:** Kunsthalle Tubingen
- **Description:** Visitors created paintings on-site, which the AI reinterpreted through the styles of expressionist masters. Nearly 200 participants contributed original works displayed as inspiration for subsequent visitors, creating a collaborative exchange between human artists, classical painters, and machine learning. A companion piece titled "1902--2012" presented AI interpretations of expressionist paintings as a flowing visual stream.
- **Credits:** Niklas Fricke, Alexander Loktyushin, Mirko Franjic, Johannes Stelzer

#### MPI Bridging Event
- **Year:** September 2019
- **Venue:** Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, Tubingen, Germany
- **Description:** "The Cross-Site Bridging Event in September 2019 brought together AI researchers from the Max-Planck-Institutes for Intelligent Systems of Stuttgart and Tubingen." The event transformed the MPI foyer into an art gallery featuring AI-generated artworks. Two featured pieces explored artistic morphing: one examined Kandinsky paintings reinterpreted through neural networks, while another explored the fractal geometry of clouds.
- **Credits:** Alexander Loktyushin, Johannes Stelzer, Niklas Fricke

### Atelier / Tools

#### Latent Blending
- **Year:** January 2023
- **Description:** A text-to-video method that creates smooth transitions between different text prompts. Leverages Stable Diffusion XL to blend intermediate latent representations and text conditioning, producing fluid transitions in high resolution. A key innovation is the use of change blindness -- by minimizing motion during transitions, the visual system cannot easily detect shifts, creating the perception of a continuous image.
- **Credits:** Johannes Stelzer
- **Code:** github.com/lunarring/latentblending (368 stars)

#### Lunar Tools
- **Description:** Toolkit for interactive exhibitions.
- **Code:** github.com/lunarring/lunar_tools (293 stars, BSD-3-Clause)

## Source: Champalimaud Foundation (fchampalimaud.org)

### Bridges to the Unknown

The Champalimaud Foundation's art-science program, launched September 2021. Aims to "intersect the two major areas of activity at the Champalimaud Foundation -- scientific research and healthcare -- with artistic research and creation." The initiative views art and science as complementary knowledge fields that can foster experimentation through interdisciplinary dialogue.

**Programs:**
- **Long-Term Residency:** Artists collaborate with researchers and healthcare professionals at the Champalimaud Centre, with resulting co-creations shared through public activities.
- **Short-Term Residency:** Complementary program offering shorter-term artist placements.

**Coordination:** Julia Salaroli (Art and Communication) and Patricia Correia (Neuroscience PhD).

**Contact:** bridges@research.fchampalimaud.org

### Metamersion (Edition 1) -- "Science, Technology, and Art"
- **Dates:** May 27--28, 2022
- **Venue:** Doca Pesca F, Champalimaud Foundation, Lisbon
- **Description:** The name blends "metamers" (perceptually identical stimuli with different physical properties) and immersion. The exhibition explores "science and art, the physical and the virtual, medicine and health" through immersive technologies like VR, AR, and motion capture. Introduced as a new program at Champalimaud, constructed at the DocaPesca warehouse as part of the Ocean Campus ecosystem, connecting "fundamental neuroscience research to human health."
- **Registration:** Free via Eventbrite.

#### Featured Works

**Augenblick** -- Lunar Ring
AI-powered real-time style transfer using deep neural networks. A camera captures the audience; algorithms reimagine the environment on displays, exploring "emergent creativity" between humans and machines.

**Haita** -- Tupac Martir
Choreography filmed, motion-captured, and volumetrically recorded, then transformed into virtual environments. Combines modern ballet, Portuguese Vira, and Mexican dance traditions with medical imaging data.

**At the Beach** -- Werner Haas
Low-resolution photographs of beachgoers transformed into behavioral notation, revealing "inner states... related to intention and emotion" through abstracted human forms.

**Polylith** -- Champalimaud Neuroscience Collaborative
Interactive installations simulating naturalistic environments using body-tracking technology and dynamic soundscapes to create convincing virtual worlds.

**Mindpod** -- MSquare Healthcare / Mindmaze
Gamified neurological therapy using an anti-gravity vest for stroke recovery, leveraging brain plasticity through experiential technology.

### Metamersion (Edition 2) -- "Latent Spaces"
- **Dates:** December 15--17, 2022 & January 26--28, 2023
- **Venue:** Champalimaud Warehouse -- Doca Pesca F, Lisbon
- **Curatorial vision:** Joe Paton framed "latent spaces" as both AI computational constructs and human cognitive territories. The exhibition investigated how technology might help access and improve internal mental and physical systems through perception and movement.

#### Featured Works

**Nowhere and Everywhere at the Same Time** -- William Forsythe
"A room with metal pendulums suspended from wire rope. Viewers are invited to engage in a choreography with the inanimate objects whose movements are programmed."

**Stellentstellen Film** -- William Forsythe
Slow-motion choreography of two dancers in interlocked formations, described as "an 'entanglement' [that] is a hybrid of choreography, film, and sculpture."

**Atavic Forest** -- Lunar Ring + Jonathan Uliel Saldanha + Goncalo Guiomar + Zach Mainen
Collaborative science-art-technology piece combining motion tracking with generative AI exploring "deep atavic memories of our species currently latent in a generative artificial intelligence."

**Latent Space I** -- A mots + Satore Studio + Lunar Ring + Eric Lacosse + Zach Mainen et al.
Multimedia exploration combining "film, installation art, and VR which explores the idea that artificial intelligence may be capable of helping people mentally heal and grow." Directed by Octavian Mot.

**Palimpsest 0.0.1** -- Eric Lacosse, Zach Mainen, Scott Rennie, Razvan Sandru
"Invites participants to a dialogue with next-generation machine intelligence carried out by exchanging images."

**Wingy** -- Hardware and Software Platform (Champalimaud Foundation team)
Interactive robot featuring "human interaction through image and speech recognition, voice synthesis and movement, enabled by the combination of a multitude of deep learning models."

**MindPod & MindMotion GO** -- MindMaze / John Krakauer
Evidence-based gaming experiences for motor/cognitive rehabilitation. MindPod Dolphin for motor/cognitive recovery; MindMotion GO for customizable game-based therapy with full-body motion capture. IZAR (January edition) for wrist, hand, and finger VR training.

#### Musical Programming
DJ GPU Panic, DJ jitterrr, Clothilde, Joana Sa, Sal Grosso, Aires, Phoebe, Usof, Odete, Carincur, Joao Pedro Fonseca.

### Metamersion (Edition 3) -- "Healing Algorithms"
- **Dates:** May 16--18, 2024
- **Venue:** The Warehouse, Lisbon (Fundacao Champalimaud)
- **Description:** "A cycle of events combining digital therapeutics, scientific research, technological development and multimedia art." Mission: improving human health through digital therapeutics. The Warehouse described as a "New Center for Digital Therapeutics."
- **Registration:** Free.

#### Daily Schedule
- 19:00 -- Round tables
- 19:00--00:00 -- Immersive installations, VR experiences, digital therapeutics, artwork
- 20:30 & 21:30 -- Multimedia dance performances
- 22:30 -- Multimedia concerts

#### Featured Works -- Art/Multimedia

**From Trauma to Healing** -- Universal Everything + The School of Life + Alain de Botton
Animated shorts + music collection guiding viewers through mental states. Minimalist choreography of graphical points accompanying compositions by Simon Pyke.

**Machine Learning** -- Universal Everything
Film series exploring: "Can humans teach machines to move?" Depicts dancer training robots; examines human-machine physical dialogue.

**My Word** -- Carme Puche More (Spain)
AI-generated audiovisual project using text-to-image diffusion models. Explores how implicit biases in AI reflect patriarchal and colonial systems.

**All around you** -- Sofia Dias & Vitor Roriz (Portugal)
Live performance using only body, voice, and simple actions. One audience member guided through the space at a time. Intimate human-performer dialogue where roles blur.

#### Featured Works -- Immersive Installations

**ConsonancIA** -- Eric Lacosse, Filipe Mendes, Leonardo Leitao, Claudia Ribeiro, Rui Rodrigues, Nico Espinoza, Lydia Neto
Interactive experience exploring human pain and AI understanding. Visual representation shifts as participants share personal information. Merges machine intelligence with human expression.

**Submersion** -- Immersive AI Systems + Lunar Ring
Captures participant images via camera; AI algorithm fuses them with fantastical worlds. Creates illusion of teleportation while mirroring real movements. Uses accelerated diffusion algorithms (1000x faster than previous year).

**Oceans of Mind** -- Immersive AI Systems + Lunar Ring
Interactive simulation of human dialogue driven by psychological states. Visitors interact with artificial agents to explore trauma, social anxiety, phobias. Creates closed-loop interaction environment.

**Altered Reflections** -- Immersive AI Systems + Lunar Ring + Joe Paton
Mirror experience where reflection gradually changes. Challenges self-perception through AI-generated manipulations. Uses distilled diffusion algorithms and control networks.

**Chatsubo** -- Carlos Stein, Mariana Duarte, Alex Farac
"Cyberpunk bar" featuring interactive AI agents (Saramago AI, Madonna AI, visitor avatars). Real-time dialogue with unique profiles and creative touches. Blends reality and virtual in retro-futuristic atmosphere.

**Manifold Time** -- Il Memming Park, Nico Espinoza, Hyungju Jeon
Collective immersive experience transforming human presence into audiovisual spatial experience. Uses sonification and visualization informed by real-time algorithms.

#### Featured Works -- Virtual Reality

**Virtual Deformations** -- Daniel McNamee, Sara Monteiro
Solve 3D challenges by sculpting virtual plasticine and directing fluid to deposits. Analyzes hand gestures and eye movements to understand underlying intentions.

**Synth Riders -- Digital Dance Therapy** -- Kluge Interactive + Fundacao Champalimaud (Scott Rennie, Prannay Reddy, Lydia Neto)
Combines immersive VR, AI, dance, exercise, and cognitive science. Provides behavioral and health insights while engaging playfully.

#### Featured Works -- Multimedia Dance Performances

**Psychoactive Surface** -- Immersive AI Systems + Lunar Ring + Nico Espinoza + Opiyo Okach
Questions whether AI can be a creative partner rather than manipulator. Human movements and music mapped to generative AI visual algorithms. Human-AI feedback loop creates emerging audiovisual dreamscapes. Features rehearsed and improvisational performances.

**Overparameterized Time** -- Il Memming Park, Nico Espinoza, Hyungju Jeon
Audiovisual immersive piece exploring mathematical representations of complex brain data. Combines neuroscience recordings with real-time algorithms and dance. Dancers help participants navigate multiple dimensions of neural data visualization.
