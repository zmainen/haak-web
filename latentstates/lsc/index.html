Latent States Camp

2023–present · Waking Life Festival, Portugal

The Latent States Camp is a laboratory without walls at Waking Life festival. Not a lab outpost — a place where ideas transform into situated experience.

We started from a conviction: certain questions about perception and consciousness cannot be answered in the laboratory, because the laboratory eliminates the conditions that produce the phenomena. A festival is not a convenient venue for data collection. It is a site where perception operates under conditions the lab cannot replicate — among strangers, in music, under open sky, sometimes under the influence of psychoactive substances, always in the presence of the unexpected.

Our method begins with phenomenology: the careful description of lived experience. We then design experiments that respect what the experience is.

Experiments
Seeing
Eye-tracking and visual perception under psychedelic conditions. Finding: reduced eye movement is linked to more visual hallucinations. Psychedelics amplify internal visual processes, they don't simply add noise.
Hearing
VR auditory detection. Finding: psychedelics improved detection accuracy rather than increasing false alarms. The signal gets clearer, not noisier.
Tasting
Does the color of a cup change how things taste? Social sharing and flavor perception in naturalistic conditions.
Soundwalk
Pairs walk a path with directional microphones and noise-cancelling headphones, refraining from verbal communication. Shared attention through listening.
Altered Reflections
AI face morphing in a mirror installation. What is it like to see your own face change?
Festival Effect
A longitudinal study: does the festival experience change you? Wellbeing measured at four timepoints across festival-goers and non-goers.
Phenomenological Interviews
88 structured interviews on subjective experience during the 2024 camp.
