About

Matthijs Oude Lohuis was a postdoc in the Mainen Lab from 2018-2020, where his research focused on serotonin neuron function in behavioral decision-making, particularly examining how serotonin activation promotes persistence and exploitation strategies in probabilistic foraging tasks. His broader research investigates the neural mechanisms underlying sensory processing and behavioral states, with work spanning visual cortex function, multisensory integration, and the relationship between arousal and cortical activity. His publications demonstrate expertise in combining behavioral paradigms with neural circuit analysis to understand how neuromodulatory systems influence perception and decision-making.
