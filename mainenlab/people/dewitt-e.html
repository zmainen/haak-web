About

Eric DeWitt was a postdoc in the Mainen Lab from 2010-2018, where he worked on computational decision theory. His research focuses on the neural mechanisms of decision-making and perceptual learning, with particular interest in how learning impacts speed-accuracy tradeoffs in behavioral choices. He has also contributed to large-scale collaborative neuroscience initiatives, including standardized approaches to measuring decision-making in mice and the development of educational programs in computational neuroscience.
