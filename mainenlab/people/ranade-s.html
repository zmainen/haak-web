About

Sachin Ranade was a PhD student in the Mainen Lab from 2000-2007, where he focused on serotonin electrophysiology research. His work contributed to understanding how serotonin neurons in the dorsal raphe nucleus encode sensory, motor, and reward events during goal-directed behavior. Following his departure from active research affiliation, his broader research interests have encompassed neural circuits involved in addiction, cholinergic systems in learning, and cortical interneuron function.
