About

Claudia Feierstein was a PhD student in the Mainen Lab from 2001-2007, where she worked on projects involving the orbitofrontal cortex, confidence, and decision-making. Her research focused on understanding how neural circuits in the orbitofrontal cortex represent spatial goals and behavioral outcomes, including detailed characterization of fast-spiking interneuron networks and their responses to behavioral contingencies. Her later work has expanded to include neural population analysis methods and whole-brain imaging approaches in zebrafish, as well as studies of adult neurogenesis and social behavior.
