About

Masayoshi Murakami was a postdoctoral researcher in the Mainen Lab from 2006-2018, where he worked on serotonin electrophysiology and studies of voluntary action and free will. His research has focused on the neural mechanisms underlying decision-making and action timing, particularly examining how serotonergic systems influence learning rates and waiting behavior, and investigating the role of frontal and cerebellar circuits in motor control and reward processing. He has since departed from the lab and his current institutional affiliation is not listed.
