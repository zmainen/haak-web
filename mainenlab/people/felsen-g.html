About

Gidon Felsen was a postdoc in the Mainen Lab from 2005-2009, where he worked on projects investigating the superior colliculus and orienting behaviors. His research focuses on the neural mechanisms underlying spatial attention and movement selection, with particular emphasis on the superior colliculus and basal ganglia circuits, as well as the intersection of neuroscience with ethics and public policy. He is currently an Associate Professor at the University of Colorado, where he continues his work on midbrain circuits and decision-making processes.
