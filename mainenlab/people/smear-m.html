About

Matthew Smear was a postdoc in the Mainen Lab from 2006-2008, where he worked on olfactory decision-making. His research focuses on the neural mechanisms underlying olfactory processing and behavior, with particular emphasis on how breathing patterns influence odor coding and how animals use olfactory cues to guide natural behaviors like foraging and navigation. He is now an Assistant Professor at the University of Oregon, where he continues to study the intersection of sensory processing and natural behavior.
