About

Michael Quirk was a postdoc in the Mainen Lab from 2001-2006, where he contributed to research on neural ensemble dynamics and hippocampal memory systems. His research focuses on neuropharmacology and drug development, particularly positive allosteric modulators of NMDA and GABA receptors for treating neurological and psychiatric conditions. He has since moved into pharmaceutical research, developing neuroactive compounds including SAGE-217 and other therapeutic agents for cognitive impairment and mood disorders.
