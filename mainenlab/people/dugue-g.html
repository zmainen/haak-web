About

Guillaume Dugue was a postdoc in the Mainen Lab from 2007-2012, where he worked on serotonin optogenetics projects, contributing to early applications of optogenetic techniques to study serotonergic neural circuits. His research spans cerebellar function, sensorimotor processing, and the neural basis of movement control, with particular expertise in developing technical approaches including optogenetics, electrophysiology, and inertial measurement systems for behavioral studies. He is now a Principal Investigator at the Collège de France and CNRS.
